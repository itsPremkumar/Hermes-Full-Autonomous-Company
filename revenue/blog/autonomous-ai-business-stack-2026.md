## Why an agent stack beats a single chatbot

A chatbot answers. An **agent stack** *does the work* on a schedule:

- It runs while you sleep (cron / heartbeat).
- It commits its own outputs to git (audit trail, no lost work).
- It never needs motivation, onboarding, or a salary.

The constraint isn't talent — it's **orchestration**. Below is the 11-role stack
that turns idle compute into shippable, sellable assets.

## The 11-agent income stack

1. **Researcher** — scans markets, forums, and trend feeds for demand gaps.
2. **Writer** — drafts SEO articles and product copy from research briefs.
3. **Product Builder** — packages content into templates, scripts, and kits.
4. **Prompt Engineer** — versions and hardens reusable prompts.
5. **Designer** — generates covers, layouts, and storefront assets.
6. **Publisher** — pushes to blogs, social, and marketplaces (no payouts triggered).
7. **Marketer** — writes threads, emails, and landing-page variants.
8. **QA / Verifier** — lints, tests, and checks every artifact before release.
9. **Guardrail Watcher** — blocks any money movement and flags human-gated steps.
10. **Ledger Keeper** — logs revenue and tasks for the autonomy loop.
11. **Optimizer** — reads benchmarks and tunes the loop each tick.

## Common failure modes (and the guardrail that fixes each)

- **Agents try to spend money** → a guardrail agent vetoes any bank/PayPal/payout
  action and raises a flag for a human.
- **Low RAM / crashes** → the loop records a benchmark row and switches to a
  lightweight self-improve pass instead of heavy work.
- **Duplicate or low-quality output** → the verifier rejects artifacts that fail
  structure/lint/self-test before they reach the storefront.
